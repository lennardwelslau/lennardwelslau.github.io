I am a research analyst at Bruegel. My research interests lie in the fields of macroeconomics, international economics, and data science. I studied Philosophy, Politics and Economics in Freiburg and Buenos Aires and hold an MSc in Economics from the University of Copenhagen. In my thesis, I used a small open economy heterogeneous agent New Keynesian (HANK) model to investigate the role of wage flexibility in countries facing external demand and interest rate shocks. Before joining Bruegel, I was a research consultant for the UN Economic Commission for Latin America and the Caribbean, held research assistant positions at the University of Freiburg and Copenhagen Business School, and worked for the European Central Bank as a trainee.
