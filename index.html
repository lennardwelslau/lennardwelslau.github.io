I am a research analyst at Bruegel. My research interests lie in the fields of macroeconomics, international economics, and data science. I studied philosophy, politics and economics in Freiburg and Buenos Aires and hold an MSc in economics from the University of Copenhagen. In my thesis, I used a small open economy heterogeneous agent New Keynesian (HANK) model to investigate the role of wage rigidity in countries facing external demand and interest rate shocks. Before joining Bruegel, I worked as a research consultant for the UN, held research assistant positions at the University of Freiburg and Copenhagen Business School, and was a trainee at the European Central Bank.
